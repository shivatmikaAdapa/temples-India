Yadagirigutta, Uttarakhand

  • Location: Bhuvanagiri, Telangana
  • Estd: 13 CE
  • Architect: Yadava Maharishi

The Yadadri temple, also known as Yadagirigutta Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple, is a popular Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an avatar of Lord Vishnu. The temple is located in the Yadadri Bhuvanagiri district of the Indian state of Telangana. The temple is believed to have been originally established by a saint named Yadava Maharishi several centuries ago. However, the exact historical origins and the precise year of its establishment are not well-documented. The temple gained significant prominence and underwent renovations and expansions over time under various dynasties and rulers. In recent years, the Telangana state government undertook a major development project to transform the temple into a grand pilgrimage destination. The temple's architecture and infrastructure were revamped, and it was inaugurated in its current form on February 17, 2020, by Chief Minister K. Chandrashekar Rao. The renovation project aimed to enhance the facilities and provide a better spiritual experience for the devotees visiting the temple.
